### Gaussian B-Splined Kolmogorov Arnold Physics Informed Neural Networks for Solving Parametric Differential Equations
**Jul 2025 - Jul 2025**

Manifold learning hasn't quite changed over different approaches for tackling universal approximation, with all the various methods that we have utilized over the years. Most notably, MLPs and KANs haven't changed a lot of what we have seen in terms of the very fundamental core application, the accuracy. However, KANs offer the perfect scientific tool for finding the underlying equations behind most of the physical systems that we can encounter under a certain boundary condition.

Every physical system can be described with a set of differential equations and boundary conditions, and if you, at some point in time, find the current physical state of the system, you can use it to predict all the future and past states of that specific system.

In this work, I reinforced the power of Kolmogorov-Arnold approximators for interpretable machine learning by utilizing Gaussian B-splines to unlock the features. The choice of this specific B-spline originates from its flexibility and universality, in terms of functional analysis. It, indeed, offered promising results in terms of accuracy and interpretability, as well as its differentiability. I have also demonstrated the universality of KANs as a generalization to converge to any mathematical approximator like the Fourier Series, Taylor Series, and others, just by changing the B-splines and keeping the knot vectors. Below, you can find an example of an application on one of the most important unsolved equations in human history.

This idea is still in its infancy, but I can see great outcomes in scientific computing and its applicable fields.

I have listed the framework for utilizing KANs with some practical PDE examples on my GitHub repo: https://lnkd.in/dzY8Q3W9

### Optimizing Restricted Boltzmann Machines via Quantum Annealing in High-Dimensional Hilbert Spaces
**Jan 2025 - May 2025**  
**Associated with INSAT - Institut National des Sciences Appliquées et de Technologie**

The success of classical machine learning algorithms in the 20s of the 21st century has engendered the expansion of numerous fields. However, it also presented a critical limitation in the form of the curse of dimensionality and computational complexity. This has led to the scientific acceleration of quantum approaches due to their low complexity. In particular, in neuromorphic computing, inspired by the success of quantum Boltzmann distribution of a transverse field Ising Hamiltonian and rooted by the principles of quantum statistical mechanics, we propose a quantum restricted Boltzmann machine (QBM) that is able to create accurate quantum deep memory representations of data via quantum annealing and quantum Boltzmann sampling following a quasistatic evolution to a stable physical equilibrium. We circumvent the limitation of nontriviality due to the non-commutative nature of quantum mechanics. This allows us to obtain a quantum Boltzmann distribution that closely approximates the target data manifold even in regimes where classical samplers struggle. Additionally, we propose a set of practices to train an annealing-based QBM in high-dimensional Hilbert spaces with a plausible tradeoff between the data representability and computational cost.

**Skills:** Quantum Computing, Statistical Physics, Machine learning

### RAYGo: Retrieve As You Go for Large-Scale Asynchronous Action Planning
**Zenodo** · Sep 1, 2024

Large Language Models are not only capable of generating text, but they are now performant on a wide range of tasks, using tools and performing actions in favor of the user's command. However, LLMs suffer from limited knowledge and deficiencies in protracted planning due to their context window length and their scarcity of attention on long context prompts, as well as compiling actions with complex input syntaxes and specific use cases. In this work, we introduce a novel approach to the asynchronous action planning of our LLM agents. We suggest a combination of Chain-of-Actions (CoA) and Retrieve-As-You-Go (RAYGo) approaches to enhance the agent's planning and action management on downstream tasks. Our approach revolves around the concept of action lookout in a vector database to retrieve actions, minimizing time complexity and enhancing the agent's performance. This has shown promising results in customer-service planning and action management, whereby this framework enabled Mistral-7B-v0.2 to outperform GPT-4 by over 29% and FILM in multi-tool calling in an information-intensive environment, both in accuracy and coherence. Furthermore, it elevated GPT-4's performance from 56% to 98% on action calling, reaching its maximum potential.

### Transformers
**Zenodo** · Aug 13, 2023

This study gives an overview of the Transformer architecture, a novel model that revolutionized the paradigm of natural language processing and related areas. The Transformer architecture eliminates the need for recurrent layers. instead, it relies on a self-attention mechanism that allows for parallel processing of sequences rather than sequential processing. This architecture makes it particularly suitable for effective and efficient training, as well as scaling, especially on data with long-range dependencies. The main components are the Encoder and Decoder modules, which are discussed in detail with respect to their functions in mapping input sequences to their output representations. The manuscript further discusses multi-head attention, which enables a model to pay attention to different parts of the input simultaneously, enhancing its capability of finding various types of relationships that may exist in data. The Transformer has become a basic architecture in many modern machine learning applications because of its scalability and the astonishing results seen in frameworks like BERT and GPT.

### Feed Forward Neural Networks
**Zenodo** · Jul 9, 2023

The course provides an overview of Feed Forward Neural Networks and their role as universal approximators in the machine learning world. The course starts with an overview of the basic architecture that underpins FFNN, which comprises the main building blocks: input layer, hidden layers, and output layer. This course emphasizes how the data flows forward through the network during forward propagation and also explains how the network learns by backpropagation—the weight adjustment process to minimize the errors. The Universal Approximation Theorem is emphasized here, showing that FFNNs are powerful tools in solving a lot of complex problems—that they can approximate any continuous function provided they have the required number of neurons and suitable forms of activation functions. Practical applications were done in detail: classification, regression, and function approximation tasks of FFNN. The course also covers how common challenges such as overfitting, vanishing/exploding gradients, and key concepts of regularization and optimization of neural networks are handled when training neural networks. It also provides background on a range of activation functions, the role of network depth, and state-of-the-art training methods. By the end of the course, students will know the basics of FFNNs and how they have been used in image recognition, financial forecasting, and function approximation, among other areas, so that they can then apply neural networks to almost any machine learning problem at hand.

### Linear-Time Sequence Modeling with Selective State Space Mamba
**Zenodo** · Jan 15, 2023

This work presents Mamba, a new architecture that avoids the computational inefficiencies of Transformers, particularly for long sequences. Unlike Transformers, which operate with quadratic time complexity, Mamba leverages structured state space models that can propagate or forget information based on the input, enhancing the handling of discrete modalities like language. Mamba also integrates a hardware-aware parallel algorithm for efficient recurrent computation. Mamba does away with attention or MLP blocks, achieves 5× better throughputs than Transformers and scales linearly with respect to sequence length. In addition to all that, Mamba has outperformed Transformers for such tasks as language modeling while showing state-of-the-art performance across languages, audio, and genomics.
