interactive · gimbal lock

Drag pitch to 90° and watch a degree of freedom disappear

Three axes, roll, pitch, yaw, applied in that order. At pitch = ±90°, the roll axis rotates onto the yaw axis. They stop being independent: turning either "roll" or "yaw" now does the same thing to the frame. That's gimbal lock, not a rendering glitch, an actual loss of a degree of freedom in the parametrization.

det(Jacobian) = cos(pitch) = 1.000 GIMBAL LOCK

Push pitch to ±90° first, then move roll and yaw separately, they visibly drive the same axis. This is the same cos(pitch) Jacobian determinant plotted statically in Why gimbal lock can never be fixed, made draggable instead of read off a chart.
